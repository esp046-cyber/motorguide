Dial Indicator Method (Rim & Face)

1 Setup: Mount dial indicators on motor shaft. One reads coupling face (axial), one reads rim (radial). Zero both at 12 o'clock.
2 Measurement: Rotate both shafts together through 360¬∞. Record readings at 0¬∞, 90¬∞, 180¬∞, 270¬∞. Look for repeatable pattern.
3 Calculate Offset: Radial misalignment = (top reading - bottom reading) √∑ 2. Target: <0.002" for speeds >1800 RPM.
4 Calculate Angularity: Face readings show angular misalignment. Target: <0.001" per inch of coupling diameter.
5 Adjust: Add/remove shims under motor feet. Move horizontally using jack bolts. Recheck after each adjustment. Iterate until within spec.
‚ö†Ô∏è Soft Foot Warning: Check all four motor feet contact base before alignment. Max gap <0.002". Causes 80% of alignment problems.

2 No-Load Run: Run uncoupled for 30 minutes. Check vibration (<0.2 in/s), temperature (<60¬∞C), unusual noise.
3 Couple to Load: Install coupling guards. Run at 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% load for 15 min each. Monitor current and temperature.
4 Documentation: Record baseline vibration, temperature, current draw. Create maintenance schedule. Label motor with commissioning date.
üí° Thermal Growth: Large motors expand when hot. Pre-offset motor 0.001" per foot of shaft height for thermal rise. Realign after 24hr operation.

üîç Common VFD Faults & Solutions

Fault: Overcurrent (OC) - Trip during acceleration
  • Cause: Ramp time too short, motor binding, incorrect motor parameters
  • Fix: Increase accel time to 15-20 sec. Verify motor rotates freely. Re-run autotune. Check for mechanical overload.
  • Prevention: Size VFD 20% larger than motor for high-inertia loads
Fault: Overvoltage (OV) - Trip during deceleration
  • Cause: Regenerative energy from load (high inertia). Motor acts as generator, charges DC bus >820V (460V system).
  • Fix: Increase decel time to 30+ sec. Install dynamic braking resistor. Enable DC injection braking at 5Hz.
  • Prevention: Spec braking resistor for >20HP motors with load inertia >5√ó motor inertia
Fault: Ground Fault (GF)
  • Cause: Motor winding insulation failure, damaged cable, moisture in motor
  • Fix: Megger test motor phase-to-ground (min 2MŒ©). Inspect cable for damage. Check terminal box for moisture/contamination.
  • Prevention: Use VFD-rated motors (inverter duty). Install output reactors for long cable runs (>150ft)
Fault: Motor Overload (OL)
  • Cause: Continuous overcurrent, poor cooling, high ambient temp, incorrect FLA setting
  • Fix: Verify motor FLA parameter matches nameplate. Check motor cooling fan operation. Reduce load or upsize motor.
  • Prevention: Set VFD motor overload to 105% of nameplate FLA for Class 10 thermal protection
Fault: DC Bus Undervoltage (UV)
  • Cause: Input voltage sag, loose connections, blown fuse, weak utility supply
  • Fix: Measure input voltage under load (should be ¬±10% of rated). Tighten all power connections. Check fuses with multimeter.
  • Prevention: Install input line reactor. Use dedicated transformer for large VFDs (>50HP)
‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Warning: VFD DC bus capacitors store lethal voltage for 5+ minutes after power-off. Always wait 10 min and verify <50V DC before touching internals.

üîê Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) Procedures

OSHA 1910.147 Compliance

LOTO Step-by-Step Procedure

1 Preparation & Notification: Identify all energy sources (electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, thermal, chemical, gravity). Notify all affected personnel. Post warning signs. Establish who has authority.
2 Equipment Shutdown: Use normal shutdown procedure. Press STOP button or turn off control switch. DO NOT forcibly shut down - could damage equipment or create hazards.
3 Isolate Energy Sources: Open main disconnect switch. Remove or rack out circuit breaker. Pull and store fuses in lockbox. Close manual valves for pneumatic/hydraulic systems.
4 Apply Lockout Devices: Install LOTO lock on disconnect. Each worker applies their own lock (never share). Use multiple-lock hasp if needed. Attach danger tag with name, date, reason.
5 Release Stored Energy: Discharge capacitors (wait 5 min, then short with grounding stick). Bleed pneumatic/hydraulic pressure. Block suspended components. Relieve spring tension.
6 Verify Zero Energy: Use voltage tester on all phases to ground. Test tester before and after (live-dead-live test). Try to operate equipment (should not start). Measure at motor terminals.
7 Perform Work: Only after zero energy verified. Re-verify periodically during long jobs (capacitors can re-charge). Maintain lockout throughout entire work period.
8 Restore Energy: Remove all tools and equipment. Replace guards and covers. Inspect work area - ensure no one inside hazard zone. Notify all personnel. Remove tags THEN locks. Test equipment.
üí° Group LOTO: When multiple people work on same equipment, EACH person applies their own lock. Last person to finish removes their lock. Supervisor verifies all personnel accounted for.

Common Energy Sources Requiring LOTO

Electrical:
  • Main power disconnect or circuit breaker
  • Control power transformer (120V circuits)
  • VFD DC bus capacitors (wait 5-10 minutes to discharge)
  • Battery backup systems (UPS)
  • Emergency generator auto-start circuits
Mechanical:
  • Rotating shaft inertia (use brake or chock)
  • Belt/chain drives (can rotate motor when driven load moves)
  • Flywheels (high stored kinetic energy - takes time to stop)
  • Coupled equipment (pumps, fans can back-drive motor)
Other Hazards:
  • Process media backflow through pumps (close isolation valves)
  • Hot surfaces (>140¬∞F - allow cool-down time)
  • Pressurized systems (air, hydraulic - bleed pressure)
  • Gravity loads (suspended masses - install blocks)

2. Motor Downsizing / Right-Sizing

When to Downsize:
  • Motor consistently runs at <50% load (measure with ammeter or power meter)
  • Power factor consistently <0.75 (indicates light loading)
  • Original sizing was overly conservative (common with pumps/fans)

3. Power Factor Correction

Low Power Factor Causes:
  • Motors running under 50% load (most common)
  • Old standard efficiency motors (higher magnetizing current)
  • Transformers with light loading
  • Long cable runs with capacitive effects
Correction Methods:
  • Capacitor Banks: Adds leading vars to offset lagging motor vars. Size for 0.95 target PF. Never use with VFDs (harmonic resonance).

4. Proper Maintenance for Efficiency

Maintenance Impact on Efficiency:
  • Bearing Lubrication: Under-lubricated bearings increase friction losses 5-10%. Over-lubrication increases windage losses 2-3%.
